# Why this one matters

Chin is not ex-Amazon. He read *Working Backwards*, applied the PR/FAQ method to his own business, and then published the actual PR/FAQ he used to align a small team on the bet. What you're reading is not a template or a sample — it's the artifact that was used to make a real decision, warts included. The rest of the published PR/FAQ canon is constructed; this one has been through the meat grinder of actual operator use.

Read it for two moves from `SKILL.md §5`:

- **Objection inoculation** — the internal FAQ is the move's home base, and Chin's version is unusually honest. Questions stage the doubts a skeptical stakeholder would raise ("how is this different from Blinkist?", "why $X pricing?", "why is the free tier justified?"), with answers that concede where the answer is uncertain.
- **Future-dated endpoint** — the PR is written for the day the beta launches, with specific success targets (500+ participants, 6/7 education score) and specific logistics (seven cases, no more than 2,000 words each, email delivery via ConvertKit, forum access).

# How to read it for craft

This reference earns its place in the library for one reason: it shows what the PR/FAQ method looks like when the person writing it cannot hide behind a PR department, a large brand, or the idea that the doc is just an exercise. Chin had to actually ship this.

Three craft lessons to pull from the doc:

1. **The internal FAQ is the most valuable section.** The external FAQ handles what the press and customers will ask; the internal FAQ handles what the CEO, the CFO, and the skeptical senior engineer will ask. Chin's internal FAQ names the sales target in a specific number (500+ participants) and the success criterion in a specific rating (6/7 education score). Those numbers commit the author. Good internal FAQ sections always commit the author.

2. **Pricing questions belong in the PR/FAQ, not a separate doc.** Chin's PR/FAQ addresses pricing justification head-on: what the free tier is for, what the paid tier is for, and why the price point is what it is. Keeping this in the PR/FAQ prevents the pricing debate from happening in a separate meeting where the strategic framing has been lost.

3. **Competitor comparisons must be specific, not categorical.** Chin names Blinkist by name, states what Blinkist does, and states what the Case Library does differently. "We're not a Blinkist" is a useless sentence. "Blinkist summarizes books; the Case Library indexes real-world instantiations of business concepts across books, articles, and news" is the level of specificity the form demands.
