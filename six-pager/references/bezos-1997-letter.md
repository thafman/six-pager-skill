# Why this one matters

Amazon attaches this letter to every annual letter that follows. The company keeps re-publishing it because it set the rhetorical template the next two decades would run on: institutional voice, long-term framing, and a parallel-objective list doubling as a commitment device.

Read it for three moves from `SKILL.md §5`:

- **Term-of-art coinage** — *"It's all about the long term"* is defined in the opening and then used as load-bearing scaffolding for the entire letter. The concept is re-invoked to explain each decision the letter describes.
- **Parallel-objective list** — a bolded, verb-first list of the decision-making principles ("We will continue to focus relentlessly on our customers...", "We will continue to make investment decisions in light of long-term market leadership considerations..."). Same grammatical shape, six items, each specific enough to be a commitment.
- **Defensive-AND-offensive framing** — the letter simultaneously frames long-term thinking as a hedge against short-term market pressure *and* as a catalyst for market-leadership investment.

# How to read it for craft

Pay attention to how the letter gets away with making a founder case inside a shareholder letter. It solves the voice problem by staying in institutional "we" throughout, grounding every claim with a stat (number of customers, number of items shipped, repeat-purchase rate), and treating the reader as a thoughtful adult who will accept a long-term argument if it is stated plainly.

Note also the structural discipline: the letter establishes the framework ("It's all about the long term"), enumerates the commitments that follow from it, reports progress against each, and closes with forward-looking direction. Background → framework → commitments → evidence → ask. That is the template the later six-pager will formalize.

# Short excerpt illustrating the parallel-objective move

From the "It's All About the Long Term" section, the items appear as bolded lead-ins to paragraphs:

> - **We will continue to focus relentlessly on our customers.**
> - **We will continue to make investment decisions in light of long-term market leadership considerations rather than short-term profitability considerations or short-term Wall Street reactions.**
> - **We will continue to measure our programs and the effectiveness of our investments analytically, to jettison those that do not provide acceptable returns, and to step up our investment in those that work best.**

The voice is measured. No hedges, no hype. Each line is a commitment the reader can hold the writer to. This is the voice-target for a `/six-pager`.

# Where to read the full letter

The 1997 letter is appended (verbatim, by Amazon's own choice) to Bezos's final letter as CEO in 2020 and is also on file with the SEC as part of Amazon's 1997 10-K. The PDF on Amazon's IR site is the cleanest public copy.
