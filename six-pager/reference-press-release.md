# Press Release

## Capital One Introduces Sprout: A Smart Companion That Helps Families Build Good Habits and Learn Together

**McLean, Va., April 2027** — Capital One today announced **Sprout**, a playful, intelligent companion that helps kids build good habits, learn responsibility, and earn real rewards — all with parents in the loop.

Sprout turns everyday routines into moments of discovery. Kids can say, *"Sprout, can you check my homework?"* and the companion uses built-in vision to recognize a completed worksheet, celebrating with light, sound, and a cheerful animation. Cleaning a room? Sprout takes a quick photo, confirms it's done, and awards **Gems** — digital points kids can save or redeem for family activities or treats. The longer they wait to redeem, the more Gems they earn, teaching patience and saving through play.

Each Sprout has a distinct personality — expressive, kind, and encouraging. It hums while waiting, cheers when goals are met, and offers gentle reminders when tasks are left unfinished. Kids can personalize their Sprout's look, voice, and name, making it feel like part of the family.

Parents stay fully in control through a private dashboard that shows progress, approvals, and trends. They can set goals, automate allowances, and track growth, all within Capital One's trusted, COPPA-compliant system designed for family safety and transparency.

"When families work together to teach consistency and responsibility, great things happen," said **Celia Karam, President of Capital One Bank**. "Sprout makes those everyday lessons joyful — it helps parents motivate and guide while giving kids a sense of pride and independence. And as kids learn to save their Gems, they start to understand real financial concepts like earning interest, setting goals, and the value of waiting — lessons that will stay with them long after the chores are done."

Getting started with Sprout is simple. Parents download the Sprout app on their own and their child's devices, complete a quick setup, and the companion instantly begins learning routines and preferences. Tasks and rewards sync automatically between parent and child, so both stay in sync without managing lists or reminders. The experience feels seamless — powered quietly behind the scenes by Capital One's intelligent systems.

"My son loves showing Sprout his clean room," said **Erika Lopez**, a pilot participant from Denver. "He gets instant feedback and can't wait to earn his Gems. It's turned chores into a game we both enjoy."

Sprout will roll out in select markets this summer, with nationwide availability later this year. Families can join the waitlist at **www.capitalone.com/sprout**.

**Capital One Sprout — Grow good habits together.**
